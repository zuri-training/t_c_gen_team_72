What are Cookies, web beacons, and APIs?

Cookies and web beacons are tracking technologies that are stored on a user's browser or device that allow your
website to remember that user's preferences. If you use analytic services like Google Analytic, you use cookies.

APIs are application programming interfaces. The Google APIs were developed so that third parties could integrate
Google services into their own websites and applications. If you use Google Maps or Google Places, you use Google
Maps APIs (also known as Google Maps Platform APIs).
