TermsCreator Security FAQS

Termscreator is dedicated to meeting our customers data protection and data security needs. This page presents our users with a summary of our practices and policies, which helps us keep your personal information safe and secure.

ACCESS CONTROL

Preventing Unauthorized Product Access

Authentication

For our customers products, we have adopted a standard password policy:

  1. Minimum 8 symbols in length.Password must have at least one digit, one lowercase letter, and one uppercase letter.
  2. Before accessing non-public cliennt data, users using the user interface for Termscreator services must first login.

Seperation of environments

To reduce the dangers of unauthorized access or changes to the operational environment, we divide the development, testing, and operational environments.

Preventing Unauthorized Access to Infrastructure

Physical and environmental security

Multi-tenant, external infrastructure providers host the infrastructure for our products. Their environmental and physical security controls are assessed for conformity with a wide range of standards.

Network Protection

In order to stop network traffic utilizing unapproved protocols from reaching the product infrastructure, network access control measures are used. The technical safeguards that have been adopted vary amongst infrastructure providers and include typical firewall rules, security group assignment, and Virtual Private Cloud (VPC) implementations. To safeguard programs that can be accesses through the internet, we have put in place a Web Application Firewall (WAF) solution. The WAF is made to recognize and stop assaults on netwok services that are accessible to the general public.

Authorization

We use safe storage solutions to keep consumer data. The infrastructure support the underllying applications is inaccessible to users. Role-based access to sensitive information is only permitted for specified purposes ( on a “need to know” basis)

Employee access

Through regulated interfaces, a small number of our skilled personnel get access to consumer data. Employee access is made possible in order to improve customer service, identify and address security incidents, troubleshoot potential issues, and promote data security.

Access is granted to employees based on their function, and all access requests are recorded. the infratsrtucture is only accessible to a selected group of employees. Databases belonging to clients are not physically accessible to Termscreator staff. All new hires are required to complete privacy and security training as part of the onboarding process and as a condition of staying on the job.
